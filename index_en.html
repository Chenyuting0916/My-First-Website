Time Line

  • Product Developer at Titansoft (2020/08 ~)

    After becoming a full-time engineer, I learned a lot, including agile development (scrum), the domain of the industry, microservices, writing APIs (REST, gRPC), and also learned how to write unit tests (xUnit, NUnit, fluent Assertion) and integration tests (specflow). I have also used Vue3.0 on the front-end.

    My code also looks better now. I read Clean Code, understood some code smells, and improved them. Although there is still much to improve, it is much better than before...

  • Internship at Titansoft (2020/02 ~ 2020/07)

    Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, I only stayed at the company for a month and then worked from home. I was fortunate to be an intern at Titansoft in 2020. Although I still wrote a lot of bad code, I learned a lot.

    I learned .NET Core MVC development and how to use MSSQL, which were things I hadn't learned in school. I also tried useful things like LINQ and jQuery and became more motivated to write good code and work with the team. It was a great experience!

  • Teaching Assistant for Programming Course (2019/10 ~ 2019/12)

    I served as a programming teaching assistant this semester, teaching Java and programming skills to students outside of my department. Teaching students who knew nothing about programming languages taught me to be meticulous and patient. Being able to teach a skill and being recognized by peers for my ability gave me a sense of accomplishment.

  • 3rd place in the 24th International ICT Innovative Services Awards

    (2019/10/07 ~ 2019/11/02) 

    Achieved outstanding performance in the research Project and was recommended by the advisor to participate in the competition. During the research, we collaborated to improve the algorithm and adopted an innovative separate structure encryption method, which revolutionized the approach for encryption/decryption. Through this experience, I learned the importance of teamwork and practical skills for implementing projects. The ability to devise new algorithms and put them into practice gave me a sense of accomplishment and increased my passion for programming. (3rd place in the Information Application group)

  • Research Project (2018/03 ~ 2019/09)

    The research project focused on message encryption/decryption. It was my first time working with others to develop a method that could resist attacks against plaintext/ciphertext. Although my partner and I had different ideas at times during the research, we were able to reach a consensus through discussions and identified bugs that the other person had missed. This experience helped me understand that teamwork produces greater results than individual efforts. Our research results improved the existing encryption/decryption mode and increased its efficiency by 17.5 times.

  • Department Student Association (2017 ~ 2018)

    Participated in the Department Student Association activities, learned how to interact with peers and collaborated with everyone. I served as a member of the activity photography group, the organizer of the esports competition, and the assistant of the department's orientation team. As an introverted person, organizing activities and socializing with people were significant challenges for me. However, these activities allowed me to make many new friends and transformed my previous pressure and anxiety into pleasant memories.
